Start at the Beginning

Unlike many others in her field, Kara never started off with the intention of becoming a paramedic. In her own words, "It started with a boy." In 2009, a newly minted High School graduate the year before, Kara looked at her employment options in a floundering economy and made a decision. Nursing school had been her original plan, but she found it harder to get into than NASA. The Army was a non-starter - Kara has a congenital hearing defect and so was deemed Not Army Material. Her first serious relationship had just ended, so she took a job at a big box store where they wear blue polo shirts and sell technology.

At the blue box store, Kara met a new potential romance, inspiring her to reconsider her employment options. Something recession-proof. Health Care. Nursing was still a non-starter, looking more bleak than ever.

After some Googling, comparing the cost of tech schools offering CNA classes and EMT classes, Kara did the math on the average salary of an EMT to a Nurse and found them comparable. She took a calculated leap and enrolled in the EMT course. At the end of the course, Kara's instructor encouraged her and a few other students to continue to paramedic school. The boy this all started over ended up exiting the picture, but Kara had found something more worthwhile, and as it turns out, a career.

These days, Kara Kitchen works as a paramedic stationed with Penn State Health, near York, PA. She and her husband lead active lives, and when at work, she spends her time answering 9-1-1 calls and running scheduled medical transports. She works with multiple agencies in a wide variety of settings: rural, city, suburbs, farm, and industrial, and she has become an integral member of her division.

As for the Future, Kara has plans to continue baking, and creating amazing wearable works of art. In the past year, she and her husband bought a new house and are working to make it their home, fixing up parts of it that are boring and builder-grade. She was also recently recommended to apply for a promotion to sergeant, as she would be a great fit for the role.
